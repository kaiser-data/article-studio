Most engineers think their next promotion is a technical problem.

It isn't. It's a *speaking* problem.

I spent years assuming better code spoke for itself. Then I joined Toastmasters — and watched my ideas finally land in the rooms where decisions get made.

Here's what carried over from the lectern to the standup:

▸ **Open with the result, not the backstory.** Your audience decides in two lines whether to keep listening.
▸ **One idea per breath.** Dense slides and dense sentences fail for the same reason.
▸ **End with the ask.** A talk without a call to action is just noise with good production values.

If you write IT for a living, the multiplier isn't another framework. It's being understood.

What's one thing speaking taught you about your day job?
